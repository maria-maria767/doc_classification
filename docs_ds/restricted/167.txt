Document Title: Sensitive Information Sharing Guidelines

Introduction:

This document outlines the guidelines for sharing sensitive information within the organization. It is important to protect the privacy and confidentiality of our clients, partners, and employees. Therefore, we must take appropriate measures to ensure that sensitive information is shared only with authorized individuals.

Guidelines for Sharing Sensitive Information:

1. Only authorized individuals may access sensitive information.
2. All sensitive information must be stored in a secure location.
3. Sensitive information should be shared through secure communication channels, such as encrypted email or secure file-sharing platforms.
4. Sensitive information should be shared on a need-to-know basis only.
5. Employees who have access to sensitive information must sign a confidentiality agreement.
6. Any unauthorized access or misuse of sensitive information must be reported immediately to the appropriate personnel.

Example of Sensitive Information:

1. Personal information of clients, such as social security numbers, addresses, and financial information.
2. Business information of partners, such as trade secrets, marketing strategies, and financial data.
3. Employee information, such as medical records, disciplinary actions, and performance evaluations.

Access Control:

Only authorized individuals with a need to know may access sensitive information. Access control measures may include authentication, authorization, encryption, and auditing. All access to sensitive information must be logged and monitored.

Conclusion:

Protecting sensitive information is a critical responsibility of our organization. By following these guidelines, we can ensure that sensitive information is shared only with authorized individuals, stored securely, and accessed only when necessary. Failure to comply with these guidelines may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or legal action.
