As we move forward with the implementation phase of Project Blue Sky, it is crucial that all team leaders are fully aware of the guidelines that must be followed to ensure the security and confidentiality of the project:

1. Access Control:
   - Access to project documentation and related resources shall be limited to team members listed in the Access Control Document (ACD).
   - Any request for access by personnel not listed in the ACD must be submitted in writing to the Director of Product Development for consideration.

2. Documentation:
   - All project documentation must be marked with the "RESTRICTED" classification header and stored in secure, encrypted databases.
   - Physical copies of sensitive project materials must be logged and kept in locked file cabinets when not in use.

3. Communication:
   - Discussions pertaining to Project Blue Sky should take place within secured communication channels only (encrypted email, secure conference rooms, etc.).
   - All communications must be documented and archived for future reference and auditing purposes.

4. External Parties:
   - No information regarding Project Blue Sky shall be disclosed to third parties (including vendors and contractors) without explicit written authorization from the Director of Product Development and subsequent non-disclosure agreements (NDAs).

5. Incident Reporting:
   - Any security incidents or suspected breaches must be reported immediately to the Security Compliance Officer.

Please review these guidelines carefully and brief your teams accordingly. Compliance with these directives is mandatory and will be monitored throughout the project lifecycle.
