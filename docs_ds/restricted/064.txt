**Employee Performance Improvement Plan**

Date: April 4, 2023

Employee Name: John Smith
Department: Marketing
Supervisor: Jane Doe

Performance Period: Q2 2023 - Q4 2023

**Section 1: Areas for Improvement**

- *Timeliness*: John has consistently missed project deadlines by an average of three days over the past quarter. This has led to delays in project deliverables and has affected team performance.
- *Quality of Work*: There have been noticeable inaccuracies in data analysis reports, resulting in the need for additional review and correction by team members.
- *Communication*: John has shown a tendency to not adequately inform team members about changes in project direction, creating confusion and inefficiencies within the team.

**Section 2: Expected Improvements**

- *Timeliness*: Completion of all projects within the allocated time frame. No more than one project deadline extension request per quarter.
- *Quality of Work*: Reduction in errors in data analysis reports by 50% as reviewed by a supervisor or designated team member.
- *Communication*: Weekly updates provided to the team on project statuses, including notification of any changes within 24 hours of decision.

**Section 3: Support Available**

- *Training*: Enrollment in a time management and data analysis course to enhance efficiency and accuracy.
- *Mentoring*: A senior team member will be assigned to provide guidance and feedback on John's work for a period of six months.
- *Regular Check-ins*: Bi-weekly meetings with immediate supervisor to discuss progress and address any concerns.

**Section 4: Goals and Benchmarks**

- *By End of Month 1*: Demonstrate improved adherence to project timelines, participation in time management training.
- *By End of Month 2*: Show noticeable improvement in the accuracy of data reports.
- *By End of Month 3*: Improved instances of proactive communication, as evidenced by team feedback.

**Section 5: Consequences of Failure to Improve Performance**

Failure to meet the expectations outlined in this performance improvement plan may result in further disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment.

**Section 6: Acknowledgement**

This document is to be signed by both the employee and supervisor, acknowledging the performance issues, expected improvements, and support provided. It also confirms the receipt and understanding of the potential consequences of not meeting the improvement plan.
