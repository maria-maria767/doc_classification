Policy and Procedure Document

Title: Standard Operating Procedure for Handling Customer Complaints

Author: James Orr Smail

Effective Date: April 15, 2023

Purpose: The purpose of this policy and procedure is to provide guidelines for all employees handling customer complaints within the organization.

Scope: This policy applies to all employees who interact with customers of the organization.

1. Policy Statement:

The organization recognizes the importance of providing quality services and products to its customers. We are committed to addressing customer complaints in a timely and efficient manner. All employees are responsible for handling customer complaints in accordance with this policy.

2. Roles and Responsibilities:

2.1 Customer Service Representative:

2.1.1 The customer service representative is the first point of contact for customers and is responsible for listening to their complaints and providing appropriate solutions.

2.1.2 The customer service representative must be courteous, professional, and empathetic when dealing with customers.

2.2 Complaint Manager:

2.2.1 The complaint manager is responsible for overseeing the complaint handling process and ensuring that all complaints are resolved satisfactorily.

2.2.2 The complaint manager must review and analyze customer complaints to identify trends, patterns, and areas for improvement.

2.2.3 The complaint manager must report the findings to the senior management team for further action.

3. Complaint Handling Process:

3.1 Customer Notification:

3.1.1 The customer service representative must notify the customer of the complaint handling process and provide them with a copy of the complaint form.

3.1.2 The customer service representative must explain the complaint handling process and inform the customer of the estimated timeframe for resolution.

3.2 Complaint Investigation:

3.2.1 The customer service representative must investigate the complaint and gather all relevant information.

3.2.2 The customer service representative must communicate with the customer regularly to provide updates on the investigation.

3.3 Resolution:

3.3.1 The customer service representative must communicate the proposed resolution to the customer and obtain their consent.

3.3.2 If the customer is not satisfied with the proposed resolution, the customer service representative must escalate the complaint to the complaint manager.

3.3.3 The complaint manager must review the complaint and provide a final resolution.

3.4 Follow-up:

3.4.1 The customer service representative must follow up with the customer to ensure that the complaint has been resolved satisfactorily.

3.4.2 The customer service representative must provide the customer with a written response summarizing the resolution.

4. Complaint Reporting and Analysis:

4.1 The complaint manager must collect, record, and analyze all customer complaints.

4.2 The complaint manager must report the findings to the senior management team.

4.3 The senior management team must review the complaint findings and take appropriate action to improve the quality of services and products.

5. Training and Development:

5.1 All employees involved in handling customer complaints must attend mandatory training on complaint handling skills.

5.2 The organization must provide ongoing training and development to ensure that employees stay up-to-date with the latest complaint handling practices.

6. Records Management:

6.1 The organization must maintain a record of all customer complaints for a minimum of three years.

7. Compliance:

7.1 The organization must comply with all applicable laws and regulations regarding customer complaint handling.

7.2 The organization must comply with the organization's complaint handling policy and procedure.

Approval:

This policy and procedure document has been approved by the Chief Executive Officer on April 12, 2023.
