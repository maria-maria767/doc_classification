Position Title: Research Scientist

Department: Product Development

Location: Merlan Scientific Ltd., 1 Kings Court, Fairmount, 13031 New York

Reports To: Lead Research Scientist, R Brown

Position Summary:
The Research Scientist at Merlan Scientific Ltd. will be responsible for conducting scientific experiments and analysis in the field of material sciences. The candidate will contribute to new product development and enhance existing products. They must ensure the accuracy and validity of data, adhere to safety protocols, and maintain laboratory equipment.

Essential Functions and Responsibilities:

1. Design and conduct experiments related to material sciences.
2. Compile, analyze, and interpret research data.
3. Document research findings in technical reports.
4. Contribute to scientific papers and presentations for internal stakeholders.
5. Collaborate with the research and development team to implement new methodologies and technologies.
6. Engage in problem-solving activities to overcome research challenges.
7. Assist in the development of research proposals and funding applications.
8. Maintain laboratory resources, ensuring all equipment is calibrated and operational.
9. Adhere to all safety guidelines and practices to ensure a safe working environment.
10. Mentor junior staff and students, when applicable.

Qualifications:

- PhD in Materials Science, Chemistry, Physics, or a related field.
- Proven experience in conducting scientific research and analysis.
- Strong analytical and critical thinking skills.
- Proficient in the use of various laboratory equipment and techniques.
- Excellent written and verbal communication skills.
- Ability to work in a cross-functional team and also independently with minimal supervision.

Working Environment:
Typically, works in a laboratory setting with occasional requirements to travel for fieldwork or conferences. The candidate must be comfortable handling chemicals and hazardous materials with proper safety equipment.

Direct Reports:
None currently, but potential to oversee future research assistants or interns.
