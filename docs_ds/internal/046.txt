1. Purpose
---------

The purpose of this policy is to define the standards and protocols for the use of email communication and the handling of data within [Company Name], hereafter referred to as 'the Company'. This policy aims to ensure the secure and effective use of email as a communication tool and safeguard data against unauthorized access and distribution.

2. Scope
--------

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary staff of the Company who have access to the company's email system and handle company data.

3. Policy
---------

3.1 Email Usage
----------------

- Employees must use their company-provided email accounts for all business-related communications.
- Personal use of company email accounts should be kept to a minimum and must not interfere with professional responsibilities.
- All email messages are considered company property. The Company reserves the right to monitor and access any emails sent or received without prior notification.
- Sensitive information must be transmitted securely. When in doubt, employees are required to use encryption tools approved by the IT department.

3.2 Data Handling
-----------------

- Data labeled as 'INTERNAL' is restricted to company staff and must not be shared with external entities unless authorized by a department head.
- Sensitive data, including client information, financial documents, and employee records, must be handled in accordance with the company's Data Protection Policy.
- When disposing of or transferring data, employees must follow the Data Disposal Procedure to prevent data leakage.

3.3 Data Storage and Access
---------------------------

- Company data must be stored on designated secure servers. Personal devices should not be used for storing sensitive company data.
- Access to sensitive data will be granted on a 'need to know' basis and will be controlled through login credentials and user permissions.

3.4 Breach and Non-compliance
------------------------------

- Any breach of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
- Employees must report any suspicious email or data handling activity to the IT department immediately.

4. Enforcement
---------------

The IT department is responsible for the enforcement of this policy. Regular audits and compliance checks will be carried out to ensure adherence to this policy.

5. Exceptions
-------------

Exceptions to this policy must be approved in writing by the Chief Information Officer or a designated representative.
