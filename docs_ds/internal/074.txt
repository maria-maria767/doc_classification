1. **Purpose and Scope**
   
   The purpose of this Health and Safety Document is to establish a comprehensive framework for ensuring the health and safety of all employees of Compagnie D'Exploitation Agricole Ltd within the workplace. The scope of this document applies to all company-operated facilities located at Via C. Colombo, Premana, Lombardia.

2. **Health and Safety Policy**

   Compagnie D'Exploitation Agricole Ltd is committed to:
    - Protecting the health and safety of its employees, contractors, visitors, and the public.
    - Complying with all applicable health and safety regulations and laws.
    - Continuously improving the health and safety management system.

3. **Roles and Responsibilities**

   All employees are responsible for:
    - Following health and safety procedures and protocols.
    - Reporting all incidents, near misses, and hazards to their supervisor or safety officer.
    - Attending all required health and safety training sessions.

   Management is responsible for:
    - Providing resources and support for effective implementation of health and safety practices.
    - Ensuring compliance with legal requirements and company policies.
    - Conducting routine health and safety audits and making improvements as needed.

4. **Risk Assessment**
   
   Risk assessments are to be conducted regularly to identify health and safety hazards. Implement controls to eliminate or reduce risks associated with:
    - Operating machinery and equipment.
    - Handling of chemicals and hazardous materials.
    - Workplace ergonomics and repetitive strain injuries.

5. **Emergency Procedures**

   In the event of an emergency, employees should:
    - Follow the evacuation plans posted throughout the building.
    - Provide assistance to co-workers with disabilities or who may require additional help.
    - Report to the designated assembly point for headcount and further instructions.

6. **Incident Reporting**

   All health and safety incidents shall be immediately reported to Toma Katja Puljak, the designated safety officer. The report should include:
    - Details of the incident or hazard.
    - Names and contact information of any witnesses.
    - Any immediate action taken.

7. **Training and Education**

   Annual health and safety training is mandatory for all employees. Additional training sessions will be provided:
    - When new equipment is introduced to the workplace.
    - When an employee's role changes in a manner that affects their health and safety responsibilities.
    - When new regulations or company policies are implemented.

8. **Review and Monitoring**

   This health and safety document shall be reviewed annually by Orta Jakovčić and updates made as regulations or company operations change. Compliance with health and safety policies will be monitored through periodic inspections and audits.
