**Hawthorne Technologies Inc.**
**Annual Performance Review Guideline**

Date: April 18, 2023
Prepared by: HR Department

**Purpose:**
The purpose of the Annual Performance Review (APR) is to provide a structured process for evaluating employee performance, setting goals for professional development, and discussing career trajectory within Hawthorne Technologies Inc. 

**Scope:**
This guideline document is intended for use by department managers and HR personnel responsible for conducting performance reviews with employees. 

**Procedure:**
1. **Preparation**:
   - Managers should gather all relevant documentation, including previous reviews, job descriptions, and any performance metrics or goals set out for the employee.
   - Employees should be asked to prepare a self-assessment which shall be submitted to their manager one week before the review.

2. **Scheduling**:
   - Reviews are to be scheduled during the last quarter of the fiscal year.
   - Employees must be notified about their scheduled review at least two weeks in advance.

3. **Review Meeting**:
   - The review meeting should last approximately one hour.
   - Managers should discuss the employeeâ€™s performance, highlight strengths, and address areas needing improvement.
   - Goals and objectives for the coming year should be set.
   - Employee concerns and aspirations should be discussed.

4. **Follow-Up**:
   - Managers should document the discussion, including the goals set, and provide a copy to the employee.
   - Signed copies of the review document must be returned to HR to be placed in the employee's personnel file.
   - A follow-up meeting should be scheduled for six months post-review to discuss progress on goals.

**Confidentiality:**
- All discussions and documents related to the APR are strictly confidential.
- Managers are expected to uphold the confidentiality of the review process and safeguard any documents relating to performance reviews.

**Acknowledgment:**
This guideline document must be acknowledged by both the reviewing manager and the employee, confirming understanding and compliance with the specified process.
