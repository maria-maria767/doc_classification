**[CONFIDENTIAL] Employee Performance Review Guidelines**

Human Resources Department

Effective Date: April 1, 2023

**Purpose**
The purpose of this document is to provide managers and supervisors with a standardized approach to conducting employee performance reviews that are consistent, fair, and aligned with company objectives.

**Scope**
These guidelines apply to all full-time and part-time employees who have completed their probationary period.

**Frequency of Reviews**
- Annual Reviews: Conducted once per fiscal year for each employee.
- Mid-Year Check-Ins: Optional meetings held approximately six months after the annual review to discuss progress on goals and objectives.

**Preparation**
- Reviewee: Self-assessment should be completed one week prior to the scheduled review.
- Reviewer: Preparation involves reviewing the employee's self-assessment, job description, goals, and previous evaluations.

**Process and Content**
- Schedule a one-on-one meeting with the employee. Allocate at least one hour.
- Utilize the company-provided evaluation form, which covers the following areas:
  - Quality of Work
  - Productivity
  - Job Knowledge
  - Work Ethic
  - Teamwork
  - Leadership
  - Goals and Objectives
  - Professional Development
- Provide specific examples to support your evaluation.
- Discuss future goals and any necessary resources or support.
- Allow the employee to provide feedback and ask questions.

**Post-Evaluation**
- Provide the employee with a written summary of the performance review.
- A copy of the evaluation will be placed in the employee's personnel file.
- A performance improvement plan should be developed if there are areas requiring substantial improvement.

**Confidentiality**
All information discussed and documented in the performance review is confidential. Only the employee, their manager, and necessary HR personnel should have access to these documents.

**Acknowledgment**
Both the reviewer and the employee are required to sign the evaluation form to acknowledge that the review has been conducted and to confirm their understanding of its content and the next steps.
