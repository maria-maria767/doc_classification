Subject: Updated Security Protocols

Attention All Team Members,

This memo is intended to brief the department on the newly updated security protocols that have been approved by the executive board on [Approval Date]. 

Effective immediately, the following protocols must be adhered to by all staff:

1. Access Control:
    a. All entry points to the server room are now equipped with biometric scanners. Identification badges are insufficient for access.
    b. Employees must re-register their biometric data by [Due Date].

2. Data Encryption:
    a. All sensitive data, including client information, must be encrypted using the new 256-bit encryption standard.
    b. IT will conduct encryption audits bimonthly.

3. Remote Work:
    a. When working remotely, employees must use the company-provided VPN and verify their connection via two-factor authentication.
    b. File transfers must be limited to our secure cloud platform. Usage of third-party services is strictly prohibited.

4. Password Policy Updates:
    a. Passwords must now be changed every 60 days.
    b. Passwords must contain a minimum of 12 characters, including upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols.

5. Client Information Handling:
    a. All documents containing client data must be classified and handled according to their sensitivity level.
    b. Physical documents must be stored in locked cabinets when not in use; electronic files must be stored in their designated secure network drive.

6. Incident Reporting:
    a. In the event of any security incident, it is mandatory to report it to the IT Security Officer within an hour of detection.

Compliance with these updated security protocols is crucial to maintain the integrity and confidentiality of our company's and clients' sensitive data.

A detailed description of each protocol and training modules are available on the intranet. All employees are required to review the materials and acknowledge their understanding by [Acknowledgement Due Date].

Please be reminded that failure to comply with these protocols may result in disciplinary action. Security is a shared responsibility, and your cooperation is key to ensuring the safety of our operations.

For any questions or concerns regarding these changes, please reach out to the IT department or your direct supervisor.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.
