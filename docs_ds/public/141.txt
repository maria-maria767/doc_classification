Policies and Procedures for Health and Safety in the Workplace

Purpose:
To ensure the health and safety of all employees, contractors, and visitors while on company premises.

Scope:
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors of Lipowska, Mark, Lizuska Hrabovskyi Dzvezdan.

Policy:
It is the responsibility of the company to provide a safe work environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors. The following policies and procedures have been established to ensure compliance with all applicable laws and regulations, as well as to promote a safe work environment.

1. Hazard Communication: Employees will be provided with appropriate information and training regarding any hazardous materials used or stored on company premises.
2. Personal Protective Equipment: Employees will be required to wear appropriate personal protective equipment as required by their job duties.
3. Reporting Incidents: Any incident or near miss involving injury or property damage must be reported immediately to a supervisor or designated safety officer.
4. Safety Training: All employees will receive safety training upon hire and annually thereafter.
5. Housekeeping: All employees are responsible for maintaining a clean and clutter-free work area.
6. Chemical Storage: All chemicals will be stored in designated areas, and proper labeling and signage will be used.
7. Fire Safety: Employees will be trained on the proper use of fire extinguishers, and emergency evacuation procedures will be posted in each room.
8. Workplace Violence: Employees are prohibited from engaging in any act of violence, threats of violence, or any other behavior that could potentially lead to violence in the workplace.

Procedures:

1. Hazard Communication: Employees will be provided with Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for any hazardous materials used or stored on company premises.
2. Personal Protective Equipment: Employees will be provided with appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) for their job duties.
3. Reporting Incidents: Incidents or near misses will be reported using an incident reporting form.
4. Safety Training: All employees will attend mandatory safety training sessions, and all safety training records will be kept on file.
5. Housekeeping: Employees will maintain a clean and clutter-free work area by properly storing their tools and materials.
6. Chemical Storage: Chemicals will be stored in designated areas, and proper labeling and signage will be used to indicate the type of chemical and any hazards associated with it.
7. Fire Safety: Employees will be trained on the proper use of fire extinguishers and emergency evacuation procedures.
8. Workplace Violence: Any incidents of workplace violence will be reported immediately to a supervisor or designated safety officer.

Roles and Responsibilities:

1. Management: Management is responsible for ensuring that all employees are trained on these policies and procedures and that they are followed.
2. Safety Officer: The designated safety officer is responsible for ensuring compliance with these policies and procedures, conducting inspections, and investigating incidents.
3. Employees: All employees are responsible for following these policies and procedures, reporting incidents, and cooperating with the designated safety officer.

Compliance:
Failure to comply with these policies and procedures may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
