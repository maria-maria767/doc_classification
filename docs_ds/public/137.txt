2. Retaliation in violation of Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964.
3. Hostile work environment in violation of Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964.

PROCEDURAL HISTORY:

The case has undergone several procedural stages, including:

1. The EEOC issued a Dismissal and Notice of Rights letter, which the Plaintiff disputed through an appeal to the EEOC's internal appeals process.
2. The Defendant filed a motion to dismiss, which was denied by the court.
3. The Defendant filed a motion for summary judgment, which was also denied by the court.
4. The case went to trial, and the jury returned a verdict in favor of the Plaintiff. The Defendant subsequently filed a motion for Judgment Notwithstanding the Verdict (JNOV), which the court denied.
5. The Defendant filed an appeal with the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Second Circuit, which upheld the jury's verdict and the court's denial of the Defendant's JNOV motion.
